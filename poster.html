Why rotation matters for GRIT
A 90° rotation changes spatial layout, not just pixel values. GRIT's grounding step predicts bounding boxes — so a rotated image forces it to attend to a different region, creating a structurally different reasoning chain. Brightness or JPEG compression change pixels but not structure, so the bbox shifts minimally. Qwen has no grounding step, so spatial change is just noise.

Implications

✓ TTS works for visual CoT
Pass@9 lifts GRIT from 29.5% → 59.9%. The ceiling is real — a perfect selector would double accuracy on these hard tasks.
✗ Voting cannot close the gap
Correct answers appear in only ~3/9 candidates on average. Wrong answers split across distractors — no single wrong answer dominates, but neither does the right one. Counting votes doesn't work.
→ Need confidence, not counts
On 48% of oracle-correct questions (GRIT VQA), the correct answer has fewer votes than the plurality winner. A verifier that reads reasoning quality — not answer frequency — is required.
Structural diversity > stochastic diversity
At T=0, augmentations for Qwen recover mostly the same questions (redundant). For GRIT, each augmentation unlocks different questions (complementary). Visual grounding amplifies spatial perturbations into distinct reasoning paths.
Oracle gap (pass@9 vs deployable):
GRIT: 59.9% oracle → 29.5% greedy
Closing this gap requires a learned verifier or confidence-based selector — not answer aggregation.

TTS Gain Across All Configs

Pass@9 lift over greedy baseline. Standard recipe (T=0.7 + augmentation) gives the largest gains. Even T=0 ablation (augmentation only) shows substantial gains.

Temperature Amplifies TTS

T=0.7 roughly doubles the TTS gain for both models compared to augmentation alone (T=0). Both diversity sources compound.

GRIT vs Qwen — Head-to-Head

Baselines are nearly identical (~29.5%). Under TTS, GRIT consistently gains more on every task — visual CoT amplifies the benefit of candidate diversity.
💡
Key insight: GRIT's augmented candidates recover different questions (complementary diversity) — Qwen's recover the same questions (redundant diversity). Grounded reasoning turns spatial perturbations into structurally distinct reasoning chains.

Augmentation Diversity — Complementary vs Redundant

When the original image fails, how many augmented candidates recover the question? Qwen spikes at N=7 (any augmentation works — redundant). GRIT spikes at N=1 — each augmentation unlocks a different set of failures.

Oracle Coverage: Original vs Augmented Candidates

Augmentation adds more unique oracle coverage for GRIT than Qwen, at both temperature settings. The gap is largest under T=0.7 where spatial and stochastic diversity compound.

Limitations

  • TTS = pass@9 (upper bound — no selector found)
  • 3B models only; larger models untested
  • OCR uses strict exact-match (~13% near-miss)
  • Voting strategy design left as open problem
