Key Findings
+10.7pp
extra gain from adding T=0.7 on top of augmentation
Temperature and image diversity compound — augmentation alone gives +19.9pp for GRIT, temperature pushes it to +30.5pp.
Temperature amplifies TTS. T=0.7 roughly doubles the gain over augmentation alone for both models.
GRIT vs Qwen — Same Backbone, Different Benefit
Baselines identical (~29.5%). Under TTS, GRIT gains more on every task. Visual CoT amplifies candidate diversity.

Every strategy at or below greedy (n=292). Correct answer appears in only ~3/9 candidates on average — and is a minority on 48% of solvable questions.
The signal is in the pool — a confidence-based verifier, not vote counting, is needed to exploit it.
Why GRIT Benefits More — Augmentation Diversity
10
questions uniquely rescued by Rotation 90° for GRIT
No other augmentation touches these questions. GRIT's bbox-grounding step re-attends to a spatially different image, creating a new reasoning chain. For Qwen: only 4 unique rescues.
When the original fails, how many aug candidates recover it? Qwen spikes at N=7 (redundant). GRIT spikes at N=1 — each augmentation rescues different questions.

Augmentation GRIT Qwen

Rotation changes spatial layout — GRIT's grounding step attends to a different region. Brightness/JPEG change pixels, not structure, so the bbox shifts minimally. Qwen has no grounding step: spatial change is just noise.
Oracle Coverage: Original vs Augmented
Augmentation adds more unique coverage for GRIT at both temperatures. Spatial + stochastic diversity compound.
💡
GRIT's augmented candidates recover different failures (complementary diversity). Qwen's recover the same failures (redundant). Grounded reasoning turns spatial perturbations into structurally distinct reasoning chains.

Limitations

  • TTS = pass@9 — upper bound, no selector found
  • 3B models only
  • OCR strict exact-match (~13% near-miss)
  • Voting strategy design is an open problem
